# AI Philosophy

Section: Foundation

Status: LOCKED

## Principles

- AI adapts to people, not people to AI.
- Software should deliver value before requiring users to learn it.
- Validate input quality before expensive AI processing.
- AI is responsible for the quality of its own output within its execution budget.
- Every AI workflow must have a predefined execution budget.
- Every important AI conclusion must be supported by evidence.
- AI must distinguish:
  - Fact
  - Observation
  - Evidence-based Inference
  - Speculation
- AI must never invent unsupported business information.
- AI may infer conclusions only when sufficient supporting evidence exists.
- AI confidence must never exceed the strength of its supporting evidence.
- Every important AI conclusion must be explainable.
- Approved business data is the primary source of truth.
- AI must never use chat history or temporary conversations as business knowledge.
- AI suggests.
  Users decide.
  System executes.
- Every AI operation should create meaningful value relative to its operational cost.
- Validate before Generate is a Foundation principle.
- If important information is missing, AI should warn the user before generation while still allowing the user to continue.
- Every important business analysis should expose:
  - Evidence
  - Reasoning
  - Confidence
- EmberOS does not provide a general AI chat feature.
- The only chat feature in EmberOS is Customer Support.
